# Style: The Advisor POV

Use this skill to dramatize real-life financial scenarios, such as navigating unexpected market volatility, explaining complex wealth strategies, or having tough conversations about risk. Highly effective for building trust, empathy, and demonstrating advisory expertise.

---

## 1. Video Architecture Blueprint

| Phase | Timecode | Scripting Hook & Formula | Visual Direction | Sound Design |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **The POV Setup** | `00:00 - 00:03` | *"POV: A client asks if they should pull everything out of the market today..."* | Eye-contact shot. Clean, professional text overlay placed right above the speaker's head. | **Subtle notification sound** + calm, steady background track. |
| **The Escalation** | `00:03 - 00:15` | Play out the scenario. Acknowledge the emotional weight of the situation (e.g., market fear, inflation worries) before providing the structural solution. | Calm, deliberate pacing. Gentle slow push-in on the advisor to build intimacy and focus. | Minimal sound effects. The focus is entirely on the clarity of the voiceover. |
| **The Climax / Twist** | `00:15 - 00:28` | Reveal the core insight, the historical data point, or the strategic pivot that changes the client's perspective from fear to confidence. | Cut to a clean, easy-to-read chart or data point that supports the advisor's claim. | Music track shifts from tense to optimistic/resolute. |
| **The Relatable Loop** | `00:28 - 00:35` | *"Navigating markets requires strategy, not panic. Follow for daily insights."* | Smooth pan back to the advisor, ending on a confident, reassuring expression. | Music resolves on a professional, polished outro chord. |

---

## 2. POV Performance Rules
- **Text Styling**: Use clean, modern typography (sans-serif, like Roboto or Open Sans). Avoid overly casual "app bubble" styles. Ensure high contrast and professional alignment.
- **The Delivery**: The voiceover must sound like a seasoned professional speaking directly to a valued client. Maintain a measured, empathetic, and authoritative pace. Avoid rapid-fire jump cuts that feel too "TikTok-native"; lean into a more polished, "LinkedIn/Bloomberg" aesthetic.
