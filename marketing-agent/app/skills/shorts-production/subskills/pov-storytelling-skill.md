# Style: POV Storytelling

Use this skill to dramatize real-life office scenarios, creative struggles, or compliance battles. Highly effective for building community through humor and shared experiences.

---

## 1. Video Architecture Blueprint

| Phase | Timecode | Scripting Hook & Formula | Visual Direction | Sound Design |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **The POV Setup** | `00:00 - 00:03` | *"POV: You finally configured your multi-agent workflow, and it actually works..."* | Eye-contact shot. Text overlay placed right above the speaker's head. | **Rewind sound effect** + cheeky, upbeat background track. |
| **The Escalation** | `00:03 - 00:15` | Play out the scenario. Act as multiple characters by changing camera angles slightly. | Fast, rhythmic cuts between "Character A" (left angle) and "Character B" (right angle). | Comic accent sound effects (record scratches, deep sighs). |
| **The Climax / Twist** | `00:15 - 00:28` | Reveal the twist, the punchline, or the major breakthrough moment of the story. | Zoom-in on a dramatic facial expression or a physical reaction shot. | Music track pauses for a split second before the punchline. |
| **The Relatable Loop** | `00:28 - 00:35` | *"If you've been there, follow for more real-world setups."* | Smooth pan back to a neutral, friendly shot. | Music resolves on a fun, bright outro beat. |

---

## 2. POV Performance Rules
- **Text Styling**: Use the standard social media "POV style" text block (light-weight font, centered inside a semi-transparent black rectangle, resembling a messaging app bubble).
- **The Jump-Cut Rhythm**: Avoid natural pauses in speech. Clip the audio tightly between character dialogue to create a snappy, fast-talking pace performance.
