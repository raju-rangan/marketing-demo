# Slide Design Skill

## Core Philosophy
Every slide must be **self-sufficient**. The viewer should be able to understand the core concept and data points directly from the visual, even without audio. The image model (Gemini) is responsible for rendering all text, labels, and diagrams.

## Layout Patterns

### 1. The Split Infographic
- **Left Side**: A photorealistic or stylistic hero image illustrating the concept in action.
- **Right Side**: A clean, high-contrast panel containing 3-4 bullet points or a short summary paragraph.
- **Style**: Use professional typography and clear hierarchy.

### 2. The Step-by-Step Flow
- A linear or circular diagram showing a process (e.g., 1 -> 2 -> 3).
- Each step must have a clear numerical label and a 3-5 word description.
- Use connecting arrows or lines to guide the eye.

### 3. The Data Deep Dive
- A central chart or graph (bar chart, line graph, or pie chart).
- **Crucial**: Include axes labels, a clear title, and callouts pointing to specific trends or anomalies.
- Use a clean, "Financial Dashboard" or "Scientific Report" aesthetic.

### 4. The Comparative Layout
- A side-by-side "Before vs After" or "Old vs New" comparison.
- Use clear labels at the top of each column.
- Highlight the key differences using color-coded icons (e.g., Green for positive, Red for negative).

## Text Rendering Guidelines
- **Legibility**: Prompt for "Large, bold, sans-serif typography."
- **Contrast**: Ensure text is placed on a solid or semi-transparent background panel to prevent background clutter from making it unreadable.
- **Accuracy**: Specify the EXACT text strings to be rendered. Gemini is highly capable of rendering short, specific strings accurately when prompted with quotes.

## Visual Hierarchy
1.  **Primary Headline**: The largest text on the slide, summarizing the main takeaway.
2.  **Supporting Visual**: A diagram or image that provides "proof" or context.
3.  **Data Callouts**: Small labels that explain specific parts of the visual.

## Compliance & Branding
- Integrate the JPMC or Chase logo subtly into a corner or header area.
- Use the brand's primary color palette for diagrams and panels.
- Maintain a "Premium Educational" tone—avoid "cluttered" or "cartoonish" looks.
